--- layout: post title: Dumpster Diving at Harvard comments: true date: 2019-09-15 --- By the conventional wisdom, people arrive at Ivy Leagues with a hint of entitlement and leave with the stench of elitism. On the other hand, I learned to dumpster dive. It's not entirely clear why I turned out the way I did, but there is a simple logic to it: The young and rich are very willing to discard.

I arrived where I am presently under particular circumstances. First, I'd chosen a cheap studio in the midst of the undergraduate sector of the university - prime real estate for picking up after young extravagance. Second, I have a semester off from teaching: no running into students. Accordingly, not a soul this side of the campus, other than my landlord, knows of my existence. I do have some pride, but under the guise of anonymity, I become very bold.

When I first arrived at my new studio, I didn't have a lightbulb, and the ceiling light was broken. There was no furniture; I sat on the ground and worked by the light of the stove vent. I've collected a nice little set of furniture since then. I'm so proud of it I thought I'd share a photograph. I didn't pay for anything visible in the image, other than the blue blanket and mattress:

In fact, it might be fun to create a running list of what undergradates happily throw away:
  • Standing lamp
  • Vacuum cleaner
  • Microwave
  • Minichest
  • Plastic storage tub
  • Bicycle
  • Square folding table
  • Mattress frame
  • A nice set of five water glasses
  • Cutting board
  • Rolling cart
  • Ikea coffee table
  • Another standing lamp
  • One kitchen chair
  • Two desk chairs
There were a few things I got for free through other means:
  • Three fluffy blankets
  • Bed sheets
  • Shower curtain
  • Mattress pad
  • Tablecloth
Strangely enough, the one thing I was unable to find, and could use: clothes hangers.
