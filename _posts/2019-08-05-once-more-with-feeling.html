--- title: Once More, with Feeling comments: true date: 2019-08-05 layout: post --- I took piano lessons for multiple years. I had two teachers; I refer to them as my German and French piano teachers, respectively. Both were were Chinese; they studied music in Europe.

My German teacher was the better pianist. I don't recall her ever missing a note. She always played beautifully. My sister, always the feminist, wondered why she didn't become a concert pianist.

My French teacher was the more effusive artist. She taught music, not piano. I learned a lot from her, though she didn't know this - I was a terrible student. I in particular remember at least a couple of times when the following occurred:

I would play some passage, and she would stop me - "No, no, no! You cannot play it like THAT!" She would proceed to either sing how the passage should be played, "da-da-DA-DEE-dum," or play the passage herself. But I was a bit older when I was her student, and the pieces were not trivial to sightread. She would be spotty with fingerfaults. It was difficult to sort out what she was getting at.

Nervous and not sure what to fix, I would just play the passage again, once more, exactly the same way. She would exclaim, "Yes, yes, MUCH better!"

Of course, it's clear what she meant. I imagine that in her head she had something beautiful in mind, like the following clip. Wait for the repeat in the solo, and listen carefully for the change:

-- Tchaikovsky Violin Concerto in D major op.35, performed by Sayaka Shoji
