--- title: Once More, with Feeling comments: true date: 2019-08-05 layout: post --- I took piano lessons for multiple years. I had two teachers; I refer to them as my German and French piano teachers, respectively. Both were were Chinese; they studied music in Europe.

My German teacher was the better pianist. I don't recall her ever missing a note. She always played beautifully. My sister, always the feminist, wondered why she didn't become a concert pianist.

My French teacher was the more effusive artist. She taught music, not piano. I learned a lot from her, though she didn't know this - I was a terrible student. I in particular remember at least a couple of times where the following would occur:

I would play some passage, and she would stop me - "No, no, no! You cannot play it like THAT!" She would proceed to either sing how the passage should be played, "da-da-DA-DEE-dum," or play the passage herself. But I was a bit older when I was her student, and the pieces were nontrivial. Her sightreading would be spotted with fingerfaults. It was difficult to know what she was getting at.

Nervous and confused, I would just play the passage again, once more, exactly the same way. She would exclaim, "Yes, yes, MUCH better!"

---

Still, I think sometimes that I know what she was talking about. Playing something twice, a small change can be powerful. Listen for the repeat in the following; listen for the change.
