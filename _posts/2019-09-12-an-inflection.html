--- layout: post title: An Inflection comments: true date: 2019-09-12 --- I came home after college. Many things had changed. This was not unexpected, but some changes were real phenomena. The greatest change revealed itself timidly. It was in my mind, I thought at first; the hint of unease I felt, being a passenger in my parents' car.

Little things revealed themselves to me over time. Blank pauses when listening to waiters at American restaurants. Reviewing mistakes during tax season. Doctor's visits.

There was a time when the relationship between my parents and I was one of unilateral reliance. I understand that in large part, a proper communication of this claim necessitates quantiative qualifiers: Individuals are rarely uniform assets or liabilities. But what I am wanting to say now is that in some other significant regard, the moment of qualitative change alone, extinguishes a light, of which until then, I was not even aware.
